Chilean Empanada

Description

Empanadas are a popular and traditional Chilean dish, consisting of a savory pastry filled with various ingredients, then folded and baked or fried to perfection. The dough is usually made from flour, butter, and water, creating a slightly crispy yet soft texture when cooked.

The most iconic Chilean empanada is the empanada de pino, which typically contains a mixture of ground beef, onions, hard-boiled eggs, black olives, raisins, and a touch of cumin. This filling combines savory and sweet flavors, giving the dish a unique taste. Other variations of empanadas can be filled with seafood, cheese, or vegetables, depending on regional preferences.

Ingredients

Filling:

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1 cup raisins
  • 1 cup chopped black olives
  • 3 large hard-cooked eggs, chopped
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch

Dough:

  • 1 cup lukewarm milk
  • 1 cup shortening, melted
  • 5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
